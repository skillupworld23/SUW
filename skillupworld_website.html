I grew up in Nathyagali — a small mountain town where education was hard to come by for boys, and almost impossible to imagine for girls.

My mother had no education. But she had something more powerful — an unshakeable belief that her children would have what she never did. She made sacrifices I am still unable to fully put into words. Allah made a way. And here I am.

Education didn't just change my career. It changed everything. My family. My possibilities. My identity. It quite literally changed the trajectory of my life.

That is why I built SkillUp World. Not because it was a market opportunity. But because someone was a wasela — a means — for me. And I want to be that for someone else.

After 13 years working across global enterprises — leading digital transformations in healthcare, fintech, textile, airlines, and public sector across Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, UAE, UK, and the USA — I realized something that troubled me deeply.

The knowledge that transforms careers and organizations is not secret. It is not complicated. It is just inaccessible. SkillUp World is here to unlock it.
